Blog Post for 2 May 2019:

For the second project in Software Innovations, we worked to develop a new software application. My group consisted of Saejin, Josh, Carson, Matt Jones, Karol, and myself. Our group was tasked with creating a web-based application that allowed teachers to upload a CSV containing test/quiz information and have it automatically formatted to be distributed to students. This solution was important because most teachers use paper exams, Sakai, or Google Docs, which don't provide as much functionality as some may desire. We had a Flask web framework to start with, but needed to implement code that allowed users to upload the CSV and turn it into something usable by those here at Allegheny. My job was to develop the method that handled information from the database. In the end, a portion of my code failed to provide the intended functionality for a couple of reasons. These relate to the challenges that I faced. First, I found that it was difficult learning the ins and outs of the Flask framework while also trying to develop a somewhat complex project. The other hardship that I faced was related to this, as my inexperience made it difficult to effectively communicate within the group at some points. As a result, some functionality in my code was also found within others' work. Besides the formation of the tests, I also worked on the display for open ended questions.
